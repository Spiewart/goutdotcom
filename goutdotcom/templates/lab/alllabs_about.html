  • Medical management of gout

    • Monitoring for side effects of gout medications

Diagnosis of Gout

The diagnosis gout is clinical, but labs help. Labs can increase or decrease a provider's clinical suspicion for gout (pre-test probability).

  1. Measure your serum uric acid. Very high levels make gout more likely (but not 100%). Very low levels make gout unlikely (though not impossible). Intermediate or normal levels don't help one way or the other. Serum uric acid drops during flares of gout.
  2. We can also measure your liver and kidney function as well as blood counts. These are important when picking medications to treat your gout, both for the flare and for long-term management.

Management of Gout

Labs are used in the management of gout to:

  1. Confirm that your uric acid is at goal.
    • This determines when you can stop taking medications to prevent a flare.
    • It also will help you and your doctor know when you are at low or no risk of having gout flares.
  2. Make sure that you aren't having any side effects from your medications.
